Project Overview

Melbourne Walking Tours was created to guide Melbourne tourists through walking tours of the city's vibrant street art scene. This project marked my entry into UX design, where I conducted guerrilla user testing to validate design decisions and improve the user experience.

The app focuses on creating an intuitive navigation experience that helps tourists discover Melbourne's hidden artistic gems while providing contextual information about the artwork and artists.

Key Challenge

Designing an intuitive mobile experience for tourists who may be unfamiliar with Melbourne's layout while ensuring the app works effectively in various lighting conditions and on-the-go scenarios.

Through iterative design and user testing, I learned the importance of validating assumptions early and often. The guerrilla testing approach provided invaluable insights into how real users interact with the interface in actual street conditions.
